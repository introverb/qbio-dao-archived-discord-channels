Geoff 5/22/2025 8:07 AM
I think real nanotech is going to need quantum. Semiconductor manufacturers hit non-classical effects, my current estimate is at 40nm, but would be interested if someone had a better estimate. But then as you get further down into the quantum zone it's going to be more and more bath-like. The Drexler approach to nanotech basically ignores quantum or tries to "engineer around it." For the Feynman vision, I think the big question is what sort of thing he's thinking about. Binhi, who's a leading Russian theorist on weak magnetic field affects, apparently has proposed that nuclear spin yields "quantum rotators" at the bottom, where different magnetic conditions would change the speed of the "rotation" of the nucleus. More rotation means that chemical reactions are harder. So maybe nature is full of little quantum motors, and the question is whether they can be hooked up to anything!

So, the only way I know of to prevent decoherence is through clever error correction schemes, most relevant of them would likely be topological in flavor. The two I am most familiar with are (1) surface codes, where one embeds a graph of qubits into a (not necessarily compact) Riemann surface and encodes logical qubits as strings of Pauli operators corresponding to homologically nontrivial paths on the surface, and (2) Adinkra based codes, which are similar and are also based on graphs, but which have more complicated rules that I don’t know as well. Is there a good study of potential examples of topological error correcting codes implemented in biological substrates at room temperature? Anything we can measure? Also, are there other forms of error correction that you think may be important in biological systems?

Clarice D. Aiello 12/30/2024 7:52 PM
We were unable to obtain from them how their fields work. They say it is a combination of "DNA repair changes", "cell proliferation changes", "cell oxidative stress changes" and "cell motility changes". All those factors have been shown independently (see big list at https://bit.ly/MFEbiology) to be altered by weak magnetic fields. We hypothesize (and have tried contacting the Novocure people, unsuccessfully) that some of their effects might be due to the magnetic field component of the oscillating field; note that those fields are small for electric fields (if I remember correctly, the cell membrane potential might be related to a much larger electric field). I don't know of any mechanism whereby tiny electric fields can actuate in biology; in contrast, weak magnetic fields can have large impacts. Alignment of cellular cytoskeleton is altered by weak magnetic fields (DC as my group has shown, or AC). I believe the electric field involved (just as order of magnitude) might be too small to perform eg the alignment. All of this to say that we hypothesize that at least some of the effects behind Novocure be explainable by quantum weak magnetic field effects.

Does Novocure address ferritin in any of their studies? Ferritin is overexpressed in cancer cells and acts like an antioxidant, disrupting the function of ferritin by exposure to RF appears to result in iron release and ROS generation, which would be damaging to cells if it exceeds what the labile iron pool can handle.

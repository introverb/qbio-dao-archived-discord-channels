I'm planning a little pilot test of whether it is possible to see the Frohlich coherence type effects found in this paper https://www.science.org/doi/full/10.1126/sciadv.abl5855 in solutions of virus at RF frequencies. Based on the frequency response of virus inactivation, I hypothesise that phonons in influenza are condensing to a low-frequency mode between 10 and 30 MHz, which could show up as increased absorption at that frequency. The basic idea is to put some virus solution in an electroporation cuvette, pump with RF from a signal generator, while probing with a VNA to see if the S parameters of the solution at lower frequencies change when it's pumped (I'll also add a signal analyser to look for sidebands around the pump frequency, and the very unlikely prospect of emission at the low-frequency mode). It will cost less than $5k for some barebones equipment (I already have the signal generator) and wet lab services fees, then I will run the experiment in November.
